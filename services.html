Our Dental Services

Comprehensive care using modern technology for healthy, confident smiles.

General & Family Dentistry

Annual Dental Check-Up

A full examination, professional clean, polish and fluoride treatment, plus oral hygiene advice to keep your smile healthy throughout the year.

Preventive Care

Regular check-ups, cleans, fissure sealants and fluoride treatments help prevent cavities, gum disease and other problems before they start.

Restorative Treatments

We provide tooth-coloured fillings, crowns, bridges and root canal therapy to restore damaged teeth and relieve discomfort.

Children's Dentistry

Gentle care for children, focusing on positive experiences, education and early monitoring of tooth and jaw development.

Cosmetic Dentistry

Teeth whitening, veneers and cosmetic contouring to enhance your smile and boost your confidence.

Implants & Oral Surgery

Dental implants and minor oral surgery carried out with modern imaging and planning tools for predictable, long-lasting results.

Emergency Dental Care

Same-day appointments are offered whenever possible for toothache, swelling, broken teeth or dental accidents.

Modern Dental Technology

Digital X-Rays

Low-radiation digital X-rays provide clear images instantly, helping us diagnose problems quickly and accurately.

Intraoral Cameras

Small cameras allow us to show you detailed images of your teeth and gums on screen, making it easier to understand your treatment options.

Laser Dentistry

Laser technology can be used for selected soft-tissue procedures, often reducing discomfort and healing time.

CAD/CAM & 3D Printing

Digital scanning and in-clinic fabrication allow some crowns, veneers and other restorations to be made in a single visit.

Implant Planning

3D imaging helps plan implant placement precisely, improving comfort and long-term success for patients needing tooth replacement.

How often should I get a dental check-up?

People at low risk of dental problems may only need a check-up every 12-24 months, while others may benefit from more frequent visits.

Your dentist will recommend a schedule that suits your oral health, but the time between check-ups should generally not be longer than two years.
